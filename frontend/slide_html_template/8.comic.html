GRAMMAR HEROES

BUILDING YOUR FOUNDATIONAL POWERS

MASTER THE TENSES

  • Present Tenses: Simple, Continuous, Perfect. Remember signal words like usually, now, already.
  • Past Tenses: Simple vs. Continuous. Actions that interrupted others! "I was studying when BAM! the phone rang."
  • Future Tenses: Will vs. Be Going To. Spontaneous decisions vs. planned intentions. Focus on evidence!
  • Pro Tip: Always look for time markers in the sentence to instantly identify the required tense without translating!

CONDITIONAL SENTENCES

REAL

  • Type 0: General truths & facts.
    If + S + V(s/es), S + V(s/es)
  • Type 1: Possible future situations.
    If + S + V(s/es), S + will + V(bare)

UNREAL

  • Type 2: Unreal present.
    If + S + V-ed/V2, S + would + V(bare)
  • Type 3: Unreal past.
    If + S + had + V3/ed, S + would have + V3/ed

RELATIVE CLAUSES

CONNECTING IDEAS

  • WHO: For people (subject).
    The hero who saved us.
  • WHICH: For things/animals.
    The cape which is red.
  • THAT: For both (defining clauses only).
  • WHOSE: For possession.
    The villain whose plan failed.
  • WHOM: For people (object).

READING QUESTS

CONQUER THE LONG PASSAGES

SKIM & SCAN SPEEDS

60s
THE GOLDEN RULE

SKIMMING = GIST

Spend no more than 60 seconds reading the first and last sentences to understand the main idea before checking questions!

SCANNING = DETAILS

Let your eyes dart over the text hunting for specific keywords, dates, or names. Do NOT read every single word!

BE A CONTEXT DETECTIVE

GUESSING MEANING

You won't know every word. Use these clues:

  • Synonym Clues: Look for "or", "that is", "in other words".
  • Antonym Clues: Look for "but", "however", "although", "unlike".
  • Example Clues: Look for "such as", "including", "for example".
  • Prefix/Suffix: e.g., 'un-' means not, '-less' means without.

TRICKY ENDINGS

-ED RULES

  • /id/: After /t/ or /d/
    wanted, needed
  • /t/: After voiceless (p, k, f, s, sh, ch)
    helped, looked, watched
  • /d/: After voiced (all others)
    played, called, loved

-S / -ES RULES

  • /iz/: After s, z, sh, ch, ge, ce
    misses, watches, changes
  • /s/: After voiceless (p, t, k, f, th)
    stops, hits, looks
  • /z/: After voiced (all others)
    plays, dogs, goes

WORD STRESS RULES

  • 2-Syllable Rule: Most Nouns & Adjectives stress the 1st syllable (PRE-sent, HAP-py). Most Verbs stress the 2nd syllable (pre-SENT, be-GIN).
  • Words ending in -ic, -sion, -tion: Stress is on the syllable right BEFORE the suffix. (eco-NOM-ic, in-for-MA-tion).
  • Words ending in -cy, -ty, -phy, -gy, -al: Stress is on the 3rd syllable from the end. (de-MOC-ra-cy, ge-OG-ra-phy).
  • Compound Nouns: Stress the 1st part (BLACK-bird, GREEN-house).
