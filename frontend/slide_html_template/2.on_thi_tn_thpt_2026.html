Exam-room time management

60
minutes under test conditions

You have a little over one minute per question on average, but reading passages need more time.

Suggested time split

  • 12 - 15 minutes: pronunciation, stress, vocabulary, and short grammar items.
  • 10 - 12 minutes: cloze test, word form, communication-function items.
  • 28 - 30 minutes: long reading passages, prioritising main-idea and detail questions first.
  • Final 3 - 5 minutes: review answers, check skipped items, and avoid changing choices on instinct.
Quick reminderStable scores usually come from stronger time control, not from forcing one overly difficult question.

How to read task prompts without losing marks

Instruction keywords

Always underline words like closest, opposite, not true, mainly about, inferred, and refers to before checking the options.

“Opposite in meaning” is not the same as “closest in meaning”.

Information scope

Do not read the options in isolation. Return to the sentence with evidence and 1-2 nearby sentences to confirm the context.

A pronoun such as this, it, or they often requires the previous sentence for accurate interpretation.

Question depth

Recognition items can be solved quickly; inference items must be evidence-based. Overly broad or extreme options are often distractors.

Choose the option that is adequately correct, not the one that merely sounds impressive.

Pronunciation: vowel and ending sounds

The -s / -es ending

  • /s/: after voiceless sounds such as /p/, /t/, /k/, and /f/.
  • /iz/: sau /s/, /z/, /sh/, /ch/, /x/, /ge/.
  • /z/: all other cases.
books /s/ • watches /iz/ • plays /z/

The -ed ending

  • /t/: after voiceless sounds such as /p/, /k/, /f/, and /s/.
  • /id/: after /t/ or /d/.
  • /d/: all other cases.
looked /t/ • wanted /id/ • cleaned /d/

Quick practice tip: group items by rule, note three familiar examples, and solve a recognition item within 30 seconds.

Word stress: recognise it through suffixes

Stress before the suffix

Suffixes such as -tion, -sion, -ic, -ity, and -ical often shift stress to the previous syllable.

education • economic • ability

Stress often stays stable

Suffixes such as -ful, -less, -ness, -ly, -er, and -or often keep the original stress pattern.

careful • happiness • quickly

Two-syllable words

Many nouns and adjectives take first-syllable stress, while many verbs take second-syllable stress. Common exceptions still need practice.

record (n) • record (v)
Study tip: group words into “word families” to remember both stress and word formation: photograph -> photography -> photographic.

Subject-verb agreement

The real subject

Ignore interrupting phrases or prepositional phrases to locate the true subject.

The list of items is on the desk.

Indefinite pronouns

Each, every, everyone, someone, and nobody usually take singular verbs.

Everyone has a different strategy.

Either / Neither

Either / Neither of + plural noun still usually takes a singular verb in standard exam grammar.

Neither of the answers is correct.
A frequent trap: along with, together with, and as well as do not make the subject plural.

Passive voice and reported speech

Passive voice

  • Core formula: be + V3 / V-ed.
  • Keep the tense of the main verb accurate during transformation.
  • Watch modal structures such as can be done and must be finished.
  • Special pattern: It is said that... / S + is said to...

Reported speech

  • Backshift tenses when the reporting verb is in the past: present -> past, will -> would.
  • Adjust pronouns and time or place expressions according to the context.
  • Indirect questions use statement order rather than auxiliary inversion.
  • Commands: tell / ask + object + to V.
English Exam Prep 2026 • Grade 12

Conditionals, wish, and if only

Type 1

Describes a real possibility in the present or future.

If it rains, we will stay home.

Type 2

Expresses an unreal or unlikely present situation.

If I were you, I would revise daily.

Type 3

Expresses an unreal past situation, often to show regret.

If she had known, she would have helped.

Wish / If only

wish + past simple is used for present wishes; wish + past perfect for past wishes; and wish + would + V for desired changes in behaviour or situations.

Relative clauses and reductions

Choosing the relative pronoun

  • who: used for people as the subject.
  • whom: used for people as the object.
  • which: used for things or events.
  • whose: used for possession.
  • where/when/why: used for place, time, and reason.

Reducing the clause

  • Active meaning: use V-ing.
  • Passive meaning: use V3 / V-ed.
  • After ordinal numbers, superlatives, and only, use to V.
  • Do not reduce the clause if the subject becomes unclear.
The students who are preparing for the exam → The students preparing for the exam.

Conjunctions, prepositions, and adverb clauses

Contrast

Although/Though/Even though + clause; Despite/In spite of + noun/V-ing.

Despite the rain, they continued.

Cause and result

Because/Since/As + clause; Because of/Due to + noun/V-ing; so/such...that.

She was so tired that she slept early.

Purpose

to V / in order to V / so as to V; so that + S + can/could.

He studied hard so that he could pass.
Quick reminderAfter a preposition, use only a noun, noun phrase, or V-ing form; this is a very common grammar trap.

Word formation: part-of-speech changes

How to identify the needed word class

  • Articles and possessive adjectives are usually followed by nouns.
  • After to be and other linking verbs, you usually need an adjective or noun.
  • A noun is often preceded by an adjective.
  • Degree adverbs are usually followed by adjectives or adverbs.

Suffix groups to remember

Noun: -tion, -ment, -nessAdj: -ive, -al, -ousAdv: -lyVerb: -ize, -en, -ify

success → successful → successfully • decide → decision → decisive

Articles, quantifiers, and determiners

A / An / The

a / an is used for first mention or a non-specific item; the is used for a specific one.

a university • an hour • the sun

Few / A few / Little / A little

Few / little suggests almost not enough; a few / a little suggests some, often enough.

few friends ≠ a few friends

Much / Many

many is used with plural countable nouns; much with uncountable nouns.

many solutions • much information
To avoid mistakes here, decide clearly whether the following noun is countable or uncountable.
English Exam Prep 2026 • Grade 12

Comparison, inversion, and emphasis

Comparison

  • Short adjectives: adj-er than.
  • Long adjectives: more + adj + than.
  • Double comparative: the more..., the more...

Inversion

  • Never/Rarely/Seldom + auxiliary + S + V.
  • Only when/after/by + inversion.
  • Not until + time + inversion.

Emphasis

  • It is/was...that...
  • Use do / does / did + V for emphasis.
  • So/Neither + auxiliary + S.
Not until she reviewed her mistakes did she improve her score.

Common grammar mistakes

Frequent errors

  • Matching the verb to the nearest noun instead of the true subject.
  • Using the wrong preposition after an adjective or verb.
  • Confusing V-ing and to V after special verbs.
  • Choosing a conjunction that does not match the intended meaning relation.

Error-fixing routine

  1. Identify the error type: word class, tense, structure, or meaning.
  2. Limit your attention to the phrase that contains the error.
  3. Insert the option into the sentence and reread the whole sentence.
  4. Record the mistake by topic for later review.

Vocabulary expansion strategy

Study in chunks

Vocabulary questions often test your ability to choose the right word in context, so record full phrases and sample sentences.

  • make progress, take responsibility, raise awareness
  • environmental protection, technological advancement

Review in spaced cycles

Twenty to thirty words or phrases per day is enough if you review them after 1, 3, 7, and 14 days.

  • Flashcard front: a sentence with a blank.
  • Flashcard back: the answer, meaning, and related collocations.

Collocations & Idioms

Priority collocations

Prioritise learning by groups such as verb + noun, adjective + noun, noun + preposition, and adverb + adjective.

do research • heavy traffic • access to • highly effective

Idioms in context

Do not memorise word-for-word meanings. Learn the overall sense, tone, and one realistic example.

break the ice = ease the initial tension

How to infer when unsure

Use positive or negative tone, topic clues, and the required word class to infer the answer.

The correct answer must fit both meaning and structure.
Each week, divide review into three main themes: Education - Environment - Technology; note 15-20 useful phrases with examples for each theme.

Phrasal verbs: learn by meaning groups

Common base-verb groups

  • look: look up, look after, look forward to, look into
  • take: take off, take up, take after, take over
  • put: put off, put up with, put out, put on
  • turn: turn down, turn up, turn into, turn out

How to distinguish the options

  • Check whether the object appears before or after the particle.
  • Check the meaning of the surrounding sentence.
  • Eliminate choices with similar meaning but incorrect structure.
  • Learn the following preposition as well: look forward to + V-ing.
She decided to take up English seriously before the exam. = start studying or doing an activity.
English Exam Prep 2026 • Grade 12

Synonyms and antonyms in context

Avoid mechanical translation

A word can have several meanings. The correct one is the meaning that fits the sentence, not the first one you remember.

issue = problem / release / edition

Check the tone

A near-synonym must match the same level of formality, positivity or negativity, and intensity.

concerned ≈ worried, not angry

Read the instruction carefully

For antonym questions, think of the opposite first before looking at the options to avoid distraction.

expand ↔ shrink / reduce

Communication functions and natural responses

Common situations

  • Invitations: accept or refuse politely.
  • Apologies: accept the apology or express regret.
  • Compliments: thank the speaker without rejecting the praise too strongly.
  • Requests or offers of help: agree, offer help, or ask for clarification.

Option-selection rules

  • The response must be polite and appropriate for the speaker role.
  • Avoid responses that are too blunt, too emotional, or off-topic.
  • Check whether the reply actually responds to what was said before.
  • Prefer natural English phrasing.
“Would you mind opening the window?” → “Not at all.” / “No, of course not.”

Error-identification: five-step checklist

Five points to scan quickly

  1. Verbs: tense, form, and subject-verb agreement.
  2. Nouns: singular/plural form, articles, and quantifiers.
  3. Pronouns: do they refer to the right person or thing?
  4. Conjunctions / prepositions: does the meaning relation make sense?
  5. Word class: noun, adjective, adverb, or verb.

Test strategy

Do not read each option separately. Read the whole sentence, identify the main structure, and then inspect each underlined part.

The number of students is increasing, not “are increasing”.
English Exam Prep 2026 • Grade 12

Sentence transformation and sentence linking

Preserve the meaning

A transformation must preserve the time reference, subject, intensity, and tone of the original sentence.

Although ↔ Despite; because ↔ because of

Equivalent structures

Master pairs such as too...to, enough...to, so...that, such...that, prefer...to, and would rather.

He is too young to drive.

Link sentences logically

Choose conjunctions based on the relationship: cause, result, contrast, condition, or purpose.

She studied hard; therefore, she improved.
A transformed sentence is correct only when it uses the right structure, keeps the original meaning, and still sounds natural in English.

Cloze test

Four blank types

  • Grammar: conjunctions, prepositions, pronouns, and articles.
  • Vocabulary: meaning and tone in context.
  • Word form: nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs.
  • Text linking: however, therefore, moreover, in contrast.

Three-pass reading routine

  1. Read the title or first sentence to identify the topic.
  2. Solve each blank, but always look at the sentence before and after it.
  3. Reread the whole passage to check coherence and fluency.
The correct answer must make the passage flow in both grammar and meaning.
English Exam Prep 2026 • Grade 12

Reading: Main Idea and Detail

Main idea

Read the opening sentence, the ending, repeated keywords, and the author's overall attitude. The correct answer usually covers the whole passage.

Detail questions

Use scanning: find the keyword in the question, return to the matching paragraph, read 1-2 surrounding sentences, and look for paraphrase.

Fast distinction

Main idea asks what the passage is mainly about; detail asks which information is true or false according to the text. For NOT true questions, check each option against the passage.

English Exam Prep 2026 • Grade 12

Reading: Inference, Reference, Vocabulary

Inference

Inference must be based on evidence in the passage. Do not choose overly extreme options or ideas added from outside the text.

probably • imply • suggest • infer

Reference

For it, they, this, that, and these, go back to the nearest earlier noun and check whether the meaning is logical.

They = students? parents? policies?

Vocabulary in context

Infer meaning from nearby words, punctuation, examples, contrast, and cause-and-effect relationships.

however often signals contrast
Quick reminderIf you cannot point to the evidence sentence, your inference or reference answer is still not secure enough.

Checklist before exam day

Final 7 days

  • Do not overload yourself with difficult new materials.
  • Review your error notebook: tenses, word formation, reading, and collocations.
  • Do 2-3 full tests in real timing and review them deeply rather than doing too many.
  • Sleep on schedule and keep a routine close to exam day.

Inside the exam room

  • Read the instructions carefully and check the test code and question numbers.
  • Do not leave answers blank; mark difficult questions and return later.
  • Do not change answers at the end unless you have a clear reason.
  • Check the answer sheet before submitting.
Mindset to keep: secure the easy items, stay calm with difficult ones, and do not let one question ruin the whole paper.
