Parts of Speech

Every word in English belongs to a specific family.

The Big Three

Nouns

A

Words that name a person, place, thing, or idea. (e.g., Ocean, Fish, Beauty)

Verbs

B

Action words! They show what the noun is doing. (e.g., Swim, Splash, Dive)

Adjectives

C

Descriptive words that give more details about nouns. (e.g., Deep, Blue, Huge)

02

The Tenses

Time traveling with verbs: Past, Present, and Future.

🫧

Time Zones of English

Past

Yesterday

Things that already happened. We usually add -ed to regular verbs.
"The fish swamm away."

Now

Present

Things happening right now or general facts.
"The ocean is very deep."

Future

Tomorrow

Things that have not happened yet. We use will or going to.
"We will explore the reef."

Quick Grammar Quiz

What is "Ocean"?

It's a Noun because it is a naming word for a vast body of water.

Find the Adjective

"The friendly dolphin jumped."
Friendly is describing the dolphin!

What tense is "played"?

It is Past Tense because of the "-ed" ending at the end.

Find the Verb

"Sharks swim very fast."
Swim is the action word here.

03

Sentence Structure

How to construct perfect sentences like a master builder.

The Golden Rule: S - V - O

Most English sentences follow a strict order: Subject (who does the action), Verb (the action), and Object (who receives it).

The whale (S) eats (V) the plankton (O).

Sentence Types

Simple

Simple

Contains one independent clause with a subject and a verb. "The crab walks."

Compound

Compound

Two simple sentences joined by words like and, but, so. "The sun is hot, so we swim."

Complex

Complex

An independent clause plus a dependent clause. "Because it rained, the sea was rough."

Connecting Words

Conjunctions are like the glue of grammar! Words like and, but, or, because help connect small sentences into bigger, better ideas.

Grammar by the Numbers

50%

Verbs ending

Nearly 50% of verbs in past tense are regular (adding -ed).

75%

Subject First

In 75% of languages, the Subject comes before the Object.

25%

Exceptions

About 25% of English rules have weird exceptions. Just learn them!

04

Punctuation

Traffic signals for your reading and writing journey.

⭐

Stop, Yield, & Ask!

Period (.) means FULL STOP.
Comma (,) means PAUSE for a breath.
Question Mark (?) means you are asking something.
Without them, our writing would be a confusing flood of words!

Common Mistakes: Do & Don't

Let's compare the correct and incorrect ways to write.

Correct (Do This!) Incorrect (Don't Do This!)
  • You're going to the beach.
  • Their boat is very fast.
  • Let's eat, Grandpa! (With comma)
  • Your going to the beach.
  • There boat is very fast.
  • Let's eat Grandpa! (Missing comma - scary!)

Steps to Better Grammar

Think

1

Plan what you want to say. Who is the subject? What is the verb?

Draft

2

Write your sentence down. Don't worry too much about perfection yet.

Review

3

Check your tenses and punctuation. Does it sound right?

How to Improve?

Read Daily

Reading books or articles naturally teaches your brain the correct grammar patterns.

Keep Writing

Write a short journal entry every day. Practice makes permanent!

Listen Actively

Listen to English podcasts or movies. Pay attention to how native speakers use tenses.

Don't Fear Mistakes

Making mistakes is the best way to learn. Keep trying and communicating!
